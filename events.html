The International

The International (TI) is Dota 2's premier esports tournament, hosted annually by Valve Corporation. TI distinguishes itself with its unique crowd funding model, setting it apart from other esports events. The crowd funding model used for events like TI is primarily facilitated through the sale of in-game items, such as the Compendium or Battle Pass in Dota 2.
Before each iteration of The International, Valve releases a digital compendium or Battle Pass, which players can purchase within the Dota 2 client. These items typically offer a variety of in-game rewards, such as exclusive cosmetics, player cards, voice lines, and more.
A portion of the proceeds from the sale of Compendiums/Battle Passes is allocated to the prize pool for The International. Valve typically sets an initial prize pool, and as players purchase Compendiums/Battle Passes, the prize pool increases in real-time, often surpassing the initial amount by a significant margin.
To encourage more sales and contributions to the prize pool, Valve often sets stretch goals and incentives tied to certain milestones. For example, reaching a specific prize pool threshold may unlock additional in-game rewards or features for all Compendium/Battle Pass owners, incentivizing further purchases and contributions.
Valve provides regular updates on the progress of the prize pool throughout the duration of the crowdfunding period. Players can track the current prize pool total in real-time through the Dota 2 client or official website, creating excitement and anticipation as the prize pool continues to grow. The crowd funding model fosters community engagement and participation, as players feel personally invested in the success of The International.
By purchasing Compendiums/Battle Passes, players not only support the tournament but also gain access to exclusive content and contribute to the overall excitement and atmosphere surrounding the event. Therefore, the crowd funding model has played a pivotal role in The International's ability to achieve the highest prize pool in esports history.
The International 10 (TI10) in 2021 had a prize pool that exceeded $40 million USD. Before The International 10 (TI10) in 2021, the previous highest prize pool in esports was also from The International. The International 9 (TI9), held in 2019, had a record-breaking prize pool that exceeded $34 million USD. This milestone solidified The International's position as the premier esports event and underscored the significant growth and impact of competitive gaming on a global scale.
Overall, the crowd funding model used for events like The International has been highly successful, allowing the Dota 2 community to directly contribute to the prize pool and play a significant role in the success of the tournament. It has become a key aspect of the annual esports calendar, generating hype and excitement among players and fans alike.
