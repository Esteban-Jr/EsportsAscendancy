What is Esports?

Esports, or electronic sports, is the competitive playing of video games at a professional level. It has seen exponential growth over the years, driven by advancements in technology, online connectivity, increased investment and sponsorship, growing viewership, and globalization. What began as a niche hobby has transformed into a multi-billion dollar industry with a global audience, featuring professional players and teams competing in tournaments ranging from local events to massive international championships with substantial prize pools. The future of esports appears promising, with ongoing innovation and mainstream acceptance poised to propel the industry to even greater heights.

1972

"Spacewar!" the tournament held at Stanford University in 1972. While it's not always categorized as an "esports" event in the modern sense, it is recognized as one of the earliest instances of competitive gaming and laid the groundwork for competitive gaming by demonstrating the potential for multiplayer video game competitions.

1980

The first widely recognized esports event is often considered to be the Space Invaders Championship, which took place in 1980. Hosted by Atari, this event was pivotal in shaping the landscape of competitive gaming.
At the time, Space Invaders was one of the most popular arcade games, capturing the imagination of players worldwide. The Space Invaders Championship was a national competition held in the United States, and it marked the first time that players could compete against each other in a structured tournament format.

1997

In the 1990s, while there were several notable esports events but the one event that stands out is the Red Annihilation Quake tournament held in 1997. The Red Annihilation tournament, organized by id Software, the developers of the popular first-person shooter game Quake, offered a grand prize of John D. Carmack's Ferrari 328 GTS to the winner.
Dennis "Thresh" Fong (Picture above) emerged as the champion of the Red Annihilation tournament. Fong's victory not only earned him the Ferrari but also solidified his reputation as one of the world's first professional gamers.

2000s

Throughout the 2000s, the World Cyber Games was founded in 2000 and quickly became one of the premier international esports tournaments of its time. It featured competitions across multiple gaming genres, including real-time strategy (RTS), first-person shooter (FPS), and fighting games, among others. Additionally, the WCG received significant media coverage and sponsorship from major technology and gaming companies, further cementing its status as a leading esports event.

2010s

In the 2010s, several esports events grew in scale and prominence, but one stands out as the biggest and most influential: The International. The International (TI)is the premier tournament for the popular multiplayer online battle arena (MOBA) game Dota 2, developed by Valve Corporation. It was first held in 2011 and has since become an annual event. The prize pool for TI increased significantly throughout the 2010s, reflecting the growing popularity and success of the tournament. point.

2020s

In the 2020s, esports experienced significant growth and evolution, solidifying its position as a mainstream form of entertainment and competition. One competition that stood alongside The International was The League of Legends World Championship 2021, commonly referred to as Worlds 2021. Worlds 2021 was the League of Legends World Championship held in China. Featuring 22 top teams from around the world, it showcased intense competition across multiple stages. The event captivated millions of viewers with thrilling matches and high stakes.

Present

The first esports events were often small-scale, grassroots gatherings held in arcades, universities, or community centers. They typically had limited resources and production value compared to modern events.
In contrast, today's esports events are massive productions held in arenas, stadiums, or dedicated esports venues. They feature high-quality broadcasts with professional commentary, analysis, and production teams, creating an immersive viewing experience for fans.
As the industry has grown, the diversity of games and genres represented in esports has expanded significantly. Overall, the differences between the first esports events and those currently happening highlight the remarkable growth and transformation of competitive gaming into a global phenomenon with widespread appeal and professionalization.
